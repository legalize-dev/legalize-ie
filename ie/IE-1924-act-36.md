# Local Government (Rates on Agricultural Land) Act , 1924

##### 1 Definitions.

**1.**—In this Act—

the expression “the Minister” means the Minister for Local Government and Public Health,

the expression “county council” does not include the council of a county borough but does include commissioners acting on behalf of a county council which has been dissolved.

##### 2 Abatement of amount to be raised off agricultural land.

**2.**—(1) It shall be lawful for any county council, if they so think fit, to resolve at a meeting specially summoned for the purpose that the amount (in this Act referred to as the amount required to be raised) required under section 51 of the Local Government (Ireland) Act, 1898, to be raised for county-at-large, union, or district charges, or charges for the purposes of a county scheme off agricultural land in their county in the local financial year ending on the 31st day of March, 1925, shall be abated by such sum (in this Act referred to as the amount of the abatement) as shall be determined by the county council at that meeting or any adjournment thereof.

(2) The amount of the abatement shall be subject to the approval of the Minister and shall not in any case exceed two-thirds of the amount required to be raised.

##### 3 Proportional abatement of poor-rate.

**3.**—(1) Where a county council has resolved to abate any rate under this Act and the Minister has approved of the amount of the abatement, the amount to be levied off each separate ratepayer in respect of such rate shall be abated by a sum which shall bear the same proportion to the full amount for which he was rated in respect of such rate as the amount of the abatement bears to the amount required to be raised.

(2) Every person who pays or has paid without abatement the first or second moiety of a rate which is abated under this Act shall be entitled to be repaid by the county council a sum bearing the same proportion to the moiety so paid by him as the amount of the abatement bears to the amount required to be raised.

##### 4 Powers of borrowing.

**4.**—(1) A county council shall be authorised to borrow for the purposes of this Act.

(2) Money borrowed for the purposes of this Act shall not be reckoned as part of the total debt of a county council for the purpose of any enactment limiting their powers of borrowing.

(3) A loan borrowed under this section shall be repaid within such period not exceeding seven years from the 31st day of March, 1925, as the county council may, with the consent of the Minister, determine.

(4) The sums required to repay the amount of the loan borrowed under this section shall be raised and levied by means of the poor rate off the agricultural land in the county.

(5) Where a county council makes default in the payment of any sum payable in respect of a loan borrowed under this section, the Minister may, on complaint being made to him by the person to whom such sum is payable and after notification to the county council concerned, direct that the amount of such sum shall be deducted from the amount payable to the county council out of the agricultural grant and the sum so deducted shall be paid to such person in discharge of such sum; provided that this enactment shall be without prejudice to the guarantee fund under the Purchase of Land (Ireland) Act, 1891.

##### 5 Short title, construction and citation.

**5.**—(1) This Act may be cited as the Local Government (Rates on Agricultural Land) Act, 1924, and shall be read and construed as one with the Local Government (Ireland) Acts, 1898 to 1919.

(2) The Local Government (Ireland) Acts, 1898 to 1919, and this Act may be cited together as the Local Government (Ireland) Acts, 1898 to 1924.
