# Appropriation Act  2004

##### 1 Issue of €71,280 out of Central Fund for year 2003.

**1.**—The Minister for Finance may issue out of the Central Fund and apply towards making good the supply granted for the service of the year ended on 31 December 2003 the sum of seventy-one thousand, two hundred and eighty euro.

##### 2 Appropriation of sums voted for supply services, totalling €33,074,174,280.

**2.**—(1) The sums granted by the Central Fund (Permanent Provisions) Act 1965 out of the Central Fund towards making good the supply granted, amounting in the aggregate to the sum of thirty-three thousand and seventy-four million, one hundred and seventy-four thousand, two hundred and eighty euro and comprising the sum of seventy-one thousand, two hundred and eighty euro for the service for the year ended on 31 December 2003 and the sum of thirty-three thousand and seventy-four million, one hundred and three thousand euro for the service of the year ending on 31 December 2004 are appropriated for the services and purposes expressed in *Schedule 1* to this Act.

(2) In addition to the grants out of the Central Fund referred to in *subsection (1)* of this section, there may be applied out of any money directed under section 2 of the Public Accounts and Charges Act 1891 to be applied as appropriations-in-aid of the grants for the services and purposes specified in *Schedule 1* to this Act, the sums respectively set out in the last column of that Schedule amounting in the aggregate to the sum of two thousand, eight hundred and ninety-nine million, six hundred and seventy-nine thousand, three hundred and eighty-three euro.

##### 3 Deferred surrender relating to capital supply services, totalling €236,967,000.

**3.—**(1) For the purposes of section 91 of the Finance Act 2004, in respect of certain undischarged appropriations by reference to the capital supply services and purposes included in a vote referred to in *Schedule 1* to this Act, there is set out in *Schedule 2* to this Act the sums deferred into the year ending 31 December 2005, and the related votes and titles in respect of each of those sums, amounting in the aggregate to the sum of two hundred and thirty-six million, nine hundred and sixty-seven thousand euro.

##### 4 Financial resolution passed by Dáil Éireann on 1 December 2004.

**4.**—(1) The legislation required to give effect to the financial resolution passed by Dáil Éireann on 1 December 2004 and referred to in *subsection (2)* of this section may, subject to the time period specified in section 4 of the Provisional Collection of Taxes Act 1927, be enacted in the year 2005.

(2) The Financial Resolution referred to in *subsection (1)* of this section is Financial Resolution No. 1 (which relates to an increase in the farmers flat rate and the livestock rate of Value-Added Tax).

##### 5 Short title.

**5.**—This Act may be cited as the Appropriation Act 2004.
