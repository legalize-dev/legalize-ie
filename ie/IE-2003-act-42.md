# Appropriation Act  2003

##### 1 Appropriation of sums voted for supply services, viz., €31,040,999,000.

**1.**—(1) The sums granted by the Central Fund (Permanent Provisions) Act 1965 out of the Central Fund towards making good the supply granted, amounting in the aggregate to the sum of thirtyone thousand and forty million, nine hundred and ninety-nine thousand euro for the service of the year ending on 31 December 2003 are appropriated for the services and purposes expressed in the Schedule to this Act.

(2) In addition to the grants out of the Central Fund referred to in *subsection (1)* of this section, there may be applied out of any money directed under section 2 of the Public Accounts and Charges Act 1891 to be applied as appropriations-in-aid of the grants for the services and purposes specified in the Schedule to this Act, the sums respectively set out in *column (4)* of that Schedule amounting, as appears thereby, in the aggregate to the sum of two thousand, six hundred and twenty-five million, one hundred and twenty-six thousand euro.

##### 2 Financial resolutions passed by Dáil Éireann on 3 December 2003.

**2.**—(1) The legislation required to give effect to the financial resolutions passed by Dáil Éireann on 3 December 2003 and referred to in *subsection (2)* of this section may, subject to the time period specified in section 4 of the Provisional Collection of Taxes Act 1927 be enacted in the year 2004.

(2) The Financial Resolutions referred to in *subsection (1)* of this section are as follows:

(*a*) Financial Resolution No. 1 (which relates to an increase in the farmers flat rate and the livestock rate of Value-Added Tax);

(*b*) Financial Resolution No. 2 (which relates to an anti-avoidance measure regarding the reduced rate of Value-Added Tax on sites, when houses and sites are sold together);

(*c*) Financial Resolution No. 3 (which relates to excise duties on road diesel and petrol);

(*d*) Financial Resolution No. 4 (which relates to excise duties on tobacco).

##### 3 Short title.

**3.**—This Act may be cited as the Appropriation Act 2003.
